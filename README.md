AAF (Anteater_Acing_Future) is a hyper-local, AI agent-powered multimodality academic and career guidance project designed currently exclusively for UC Irvine students. Born out of the frustration of transitioning between rigorous programming courses (like ICS 31 to ICS 32), it goes beyond generic AI wrappers. Instead, it focuses on highly specific academic pain points—such as local IDE deployments and syllabus-specific strategies—to actively combat student imposter syndrome.

What sets this platform apart is its dual-sided ecosystem. I architected a dynamic, conditional onboarding flow that seamlessly segments users into two distinct roles: "Learners" seeking guidance and "Seniors" offering expertise. This ensures a zero-friction, progressive user experience while establishing the foundation for a targeted community dashboard.

To solve the cold-start problem typical of community platforms, I designed an incentive-driven User-Generated Content (UGC) loop. By combining intrinsic community values with extrinsic perks—such as resume referrals and LinkedIn connections for active mentors—the platform builds a self-sustaining data flywheel. This high-quality, localized data is structured to power an advanced Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) AI agent.

From a technical perspective, I built this Minimum Viable Product (MVP) from the ground up as a full-stack web application. The frontend utilizes progressive disclosure, empathy-driven microcopy, and dynamic context-awareness (such as smart placeholders reading user selections). Meanwhile, the Python/Flask backend robustly handles stateful routing and data collection, demonstrating rapid engineering iteration and a strong product sense.
